After arriving in Japan, Interns, Intra Company Transferee, and new employed foreign employees are required to complete a few important administrative matters in collaboration with the HR Administrative Department.

Residence Card

All foreign nationals residing in Japan with resident status for the mid-to long-term (i.e. more than 90 days) will be issued a Residence Card upon arrival in Japan.
New foreign employees arrive via Haneda (TOKYO), Narita (TOKYO)airport, their Residence Card will be issued at the airport.

Residence Card (Zairyu Kaado)

NOTE: You must carry your Residence Card at all times. The Japanese police often conduct card checks on foreigners in public spaces and have the right to arrest any foreigners who cannot show their Residence Cards. So, make sure you have the residence card with you when you go out.

Notification of Place of Residence

All foreign nationals who are staying in Japan for a period of over 3 months are required to register their residence at local city hall respectively (i.e. Aikawa City Hall) within 14 days of moving into the place of residence. This is called “Notification of Place of Residence (tennyu-tenshutsu todoke: 転入・転出届)”.

Residence Registration

In order to complete this process new foreign employee together with the HR have to notify the city hall by registering his/her residence. Documents required the following:
(a)Passport
(b) Residence Card

Paper My number Notification Card

Once you have registered your address at the municipal office, your “My Number” Notification will be sent to your registered address within 2-3 weeks. “My Number” is a social security and Tax Number System. Please refer to the information below about the “My Number” system and how it’s used.

The notification will arrive in an envelope (pictured above). Within the envelope your will find a green card with the word “通知カード”(tsuuchi ka-do)meaning “notification card” written at the top (see image above).

What is an Individual My Number Card?

The individual Number System (commonly known as “My Number”) is a unique 12-digit number that is assigned to each person who possesses a Resident Record. To prevent persons being identified by their numbers, the numbers are applied with no relation to identifiable factors such as home address or date of birth, etc. The My Number system offers several advantages. It helps realize a fairer and more just society as well enhances the convenience of the nation’s citizens. It also helps improve administrative efficiency.

My Number Card Expiration

Paper My Number Notice Card – NO extension is required.
Plastic My Number Card - same as expiration date of the residence card.
NOTE: If you forget to extend and your My Number card expires, your card becomes invalid and can no longer be used as an ID card. It is not illegal itself, but the card expiration date can no longer be extended. Only way to recover is to re-issue a new card, which costs a fee and takes time, e.g. a few months. To avoid such annoyance, it is recommended to extend it before it is expired.

Setting Up a Bank Account

After your arrival you will have an opportunity to open a Japanese bank account at JP post Bank with HR support.
You will be given an account passbook at the same time as you open an account. When opening an account, you can also get an ATM card. It usually takes about a week for the ATM card to be made. It will be mailed to your address. Normal banking hours are 9 AM to 3 PM, from Monday to Friday, but ATMs can be used after that and the available time depends on banks.

Most commonly use Bank is Yucho (post office) account. The largest post office in a city or region is open for extended hours. For those who are unable to get to the bank branch physically we recommend opening a bank account at Shinsei Bank.

For the Shinsei Bank, You can get the application on the internet, and apply for this account by mail, so you don’t have to leave from work early to open a bank account. However, to open a bank account at Shinsei Bank, you will need a proof document of residence called “Juumin Hyo” (住民票) or original copy of a utility bill. You can get “Juumin Hyo” (住民票) at a local city office and it costs about 400 yen. If you open an account at Shinsei Bank, there is NO ATM withdrawal fee from Seven Bank ATMs, E-net ATMs, Lawson ATMs and AEON Bank ATMs or post office at any time.

When Moving / Changing Address

Before moving from your current municipality to a different municipality, you must submit a Moving-Out notification (jyumin idou todoke) to your current City Hall. This notification will be inscribed on the back of your Residence Card and used as a base for your National Health Insurance. This notification can be submitted up to 14 days before moving or after moving (by mail). After you submit this document, you will receive a Certificate of Moving-Out, which you must submit to your new municipality (City Hall) within 14 days along with a Moving-In Notification. Your new address will be written on the back of your Residence Card.

HR does not take care of the notice of change of address on your behalf. Please be aware of this requirement and report the change of residence to the city hall(s). However, please inform HR if you couldn’t manage to do it by yourself.

What should I do if I Move to different Address?

Go to the City Office to submit a Notification.
Changing Address inside the same Municipality.
File a change of address notification to the Municipals Office where you reside within 14 days after you move.

Taxation

Every resident in Japan is required to pay taxes regardless of nationality. When roughly classified, there are the national taxes and local taxes.
Tax levied on one's income is roughly classified as Income Tax (Shotoku Zei), which is levied by the central government, and Resident Tax (Shimin Zei), which is levied by the prefecture or local government. These are paid separately. A foreign national is required to pay taxes the same as a Japanese national.
Income tax is withheld from their monthly salary. The bill for Resident Tax is posted by the prefecture or local government. Resident Tax is levied four times a year (Jun/Aug/Oct/Jan).
However, if you didn’t work in Japan the previous year, Resident Tax won’t be levied.

IncomeTax (所得税)

Income tax is levied on a person's income for a year from January through December by the central government. For salaried workers, income tax is automatically withheld from their monthly salary. (This is called "withholding tax at the source" (gensen choshu), or "payroll deduction"(kyuryo tenbiki). Tax limits and rates vary depending on whether a foreigner is a resident or non -resident (a resident is one who has an address in Japan, or who has lived continuously in Japan for 1 year or more). Foreign residents are required to pay the same tax rates as ordinary Japanese. In principle, non- residents are required to pay a 20% tax on their income, unless they qualify for a tax exemption under a tax treaty.

Withholding Tax at the Source Statement (源泉徴収票 =Gensen Choshu hyo)

The withholding tax at the Source Statement indicates the amount of income tax you have paid from January through December of the stated year and is provided by one's employer by the end of January of the following year. If employment has been terminated, please get your Withholding Tax at the Source Statement from the previous employer. The Withholding Tax at the Source Statement is proof of payment of tax. Since it is required during application for renewal of one's resident status, year end adjustments and filing a tax return, it is important to keep it safe.
