Overtime Work

The company may have an employee to work beyond the working hours (overtime) or have them work during the Holiday when it is necessary due to certain business circumstances. The company shall give compensatory leave within 4 weeks in case an employee works for more than 8 hours on holidays.

The company may also instruct the employee to work on a day specified as a day off and switch it to one of the weekdays in the same week starting from Saturday.

Attendance Control

In terms of Attendance control, the start and end time of work, arriving late or leaving early during the business hours should be recorded in the system by Employees themselves.

An employee must notify their Immediate Supervisor in advance and obtain approval when he/she will be arriving late, leaving early, absent for a full day, absent for a part day for personal or unavoidable reasons during the working hours. However, in the case where he/she could not notify prior to such event for unforeseeable reasons, he/she must notify immediately after the event and obtain approval.

Annual Leave

Annual leave is entitled based on the following for a year starting from March 16.

(1) New Graduates :　10 days

(2) Employees who have been working for over a year and the attendance rate is over 80% :　13 days

(3) Employees who have been working for more than 2 years :　See below

Note: If the attendance rate for entire working period did not reach 80%, the same number of annual leave for the previous year will be given.


(4) Employees joined the company in the middle of the year:　See below

Note: In the case where an employee did not reach 80% attendance, the company is not obligated to grant the annual paid leave for the following fiscal year. In such event, when such employee reaches 80% or more in attendance for that year in which he/she did not receive annual paid leave, the company must grant the annual paid leave for the following fiscal year corresponding to the length of his/her service.

※An employee is entitled to carry over the unused portion of his/her annual paid leave from the preceding year up to 2 years from the day such paid leave was granted.

Hourly basis usage grants for Annual Paid Leave – employee shall have the annual paid leave that can be taken on an hourly basis (1 hour) .

Basically, employees need to request their respective supervisors’ approval in advance before taking annual paid leave unless it is caused by an inevitable reason.

Special Leave

Employees are also entitled to take special leave as below:

(1) Bereavement Leave

1-1) Death of a father, mother, spouse, or child:

5 Days to the Chief Mourner
4 Days to the Bereaved

1-2) Death of a grandparent, grandchild, sibling, child’s spouse, or spouse’s parent:

3 Days to the Chief Mourner
2 Days to the Bereaved

1-3) Death of a Spouse of Employee’s Sibling:　2 Days

1-4) Death of a third Degree Relative (Uncle, Aunt, Cousins) Sibling’s Spouses and Other Relatives:　1 Day
Note: 1 day of additional leave is eligible if it takes more than 12 hours for one-way travel.

Special leave is not applied in case employees do not attend related ceremonies.




(2) Employee’s Marriage:　5 Days

(3) Childbirth:　2 Days

(4) Menstrual Leave (Female Employees) ※only for severe cases :　　Up to 2 days a month

(5) The 1st memorial service for the death of a father or mother:　1 Day

(6) Renewal of Working Permission (Only to Employees Work in the Company for No More Than 3 Years After Graduation)

6-1) Application for Extension of Period to Stay:　1 Day
6-2) Collecting Renewed Residence Card:　1 Day




Other special leaves applied as prescribed in working regulation.

Leave of Absence

The company may provide a leave of absence in case an employee corresponds to any of the following conditions:

1) If an employee request for a leave due to unavoidable reason and the company approves it.

2) In the case where an employee has been absent due to injury or illness outside the course of duties for more than 100 days within 1 year and he/she requires further treatment for full recovery.

3) The company finds it would bring obstacles to business because an employee enters the government/public service.

4) Other compelling cases would be judged based on an individual basis.


Conditions during the leave of absence is below:
- Length of Leave:　1 year
- Salary and Allowance:　Unpaid
- Service Years:　Not Counted
- Termination:　Employment is terminated when both the company and an employee agreed not to return to work when the leave period ends.

Dismissal & Resignation

The company may dismiss an employee if he/she falls under one of the following categories:

1) The company determines it is inappropriate to keep an employee to work because of the health issues based from the diagnosis of the Doctor.

2) The company is required to curtail their business operation or to close down part of their business operations due to business circumstances or other unavoidable reasons.

3) Other compelling cases would be judged based on an individual basis.

The company must notify the employee a minimum of 30 days in advance. In the case where an employer does not notify the employee, the company must pay a dismissal allowance equivalent to a minimum of 30 days average wage. However, the number of days required prior to such notice can be compensated for by the corresponding amount of dismissal allowance.

If an Employee wishes to resign, the employee must submit a resignation letter to the company through the supervisor at least 2 weeks in advance for the termination of employment and keep being engaged in the current duties until the company’s acceptance. This policy is not applied in case the company does not accept the resignation offer after 14 days pass from the date of submission.

The fixed retirement age shall be 60 years of age and a company rehires a retiree who wishes to continue to work based on the relevant regulation.



The qualification as employees is lost by the following reasons:

1) In case of death
2) In the case where an employee submits the request for retirement and the company approves.
3) Disciplinary dismissal
4) Termination of leave of absence without reinstatement
5) Dismissal
6) In the case where a fixed term employment contract expires
7) In the case where an employee is missing or unreachable after 1 month without any contact.
